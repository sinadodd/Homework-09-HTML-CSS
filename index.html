Latitude in Relation to Weather

In this project, during the API section of our course, we made a Python script to pull a few different aspects of the weather in more than 500 cities all over the world. We used the OpenWeatherMap API to get all the data, which can be seen on the Data page. Then we built scatter plots, with latitude as the x-axis to easily visualize the weather in relation to location. Select a visualization to see more, or go to the Comparison page to see all four plots at once.

Data was collected on Jan 5th, 2017. This means the Northern Hemisphere was in Winter, and the Southern Hemisphere was in Summer. Also, it is important to keep in mind that there is more habitable land in the Northern Hemisphere, so there will be more data points north of the Equator, and there will also be points in the North that are closer to the pole, than in the South.

Finally, remember that half the world is in day when this data is pulled and we did not choose cities with a balance of longitudes.
